# M1-R005 — Independence and Autonomy Deep Dive

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R005
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved
- Depends on: M1-R001–M1-R004

## Scope
This unit separates traditional Number 1 themes of independence and self-direction from isolation, selfishness, refusal of help, and dominance. Birth-number prediction remains scientifically not established.

## Canonical dimensions
1. **Choice ownership** — acting from personally endorsed reasons.
2. **Independent judgment** — forming a view before conforming to group pressure.
3. **Help-compatible autonomy** — accepting support without surrendering agency.
4. **Boundary clarity** — stating preferences and limits respectfully.
5. **Interdependence** — combining self-direction with mutual reliance.
6. **Responsibility acceptance** — owning consequences of chosen action.

## Expression model
| Dimension | Balanced | Overextended | Underexpressed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Choice ownership | Values-aligned decisions | Rejects input reflexively | Lets others decide to avoid responsibility |
| Independent judgment | Evaluates evidence directly | Contrarian for identity | Conforms despite disagreement |
| Help-compatible autonomy | Seeks useful advice | Treats help as weakness | Depends on reassurance |
| Boundaries | Clear and respectful | Rigid or controlling | Vague, apologetic or inconsistent |
| Interdependence | Collaborates without self-erasure | Works alone unnecessarily | Loses own priorities in the group |
| Responsibility | Owns decisions and repairs errors | Over-controls outcomes | Blames circumstances or authority |

## Triggers
Micromanagement, public correction, dependency, unclear authority, repeated interference, loss of decision rights, and environments where initiative is punished.

## Protective factors
Decision criteria, consultation before commitment, explicit role boundaries, reversible experiments, delegated ownership, reflective pauses, and trusted feedback.

## App-safe rules
- Do not label the user selfish, antisocial, avoidant, narcissistic, or incapable of partnership.
- Do not equate autonomy with living alone or rejecting authority.
- Present independence as a reflection theme, not a verified trait.

## Reflection prompts
- Where do you want freedom, and where do you genuinely benefit from guidance?
- When does advice feel useful, and when does it feel like control?
- Can you accept help while keeping final ownership of your decision?

## Growth target
Move from defensive independence toward self-directed interdependence: clear choices, open feedback, shared responsibility, and flexible support.
