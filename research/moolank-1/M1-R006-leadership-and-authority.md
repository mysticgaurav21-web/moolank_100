# M1-R006 — Leadership and Authority Deep Dive

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R006
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved

## Boundary
Number 1 traditions frequently associate the number with command, authority and leadership. The app must never state that every Moolank 1 person is a born or superior leader. Leadership is a learnable, context-dependent process involving competence, trust, ethics, communication and followership.

## Leadership dimensions
1. Direction setting
2. Decision ownership
3. Ethical use of authority
4. Listening and inclusion
5. Delegation
6. Accountability
7. Conflict stewardship
8. Credit sharing
9. Crisis steadiness
10. Developing others

## Expression model
- **Balanced:** initiates direction, explains decisions, invites relevant input, delegates outcomes, owns errors, protects team dignity, shares credit.
- **Overextended:** commands prematurely, centralizes decisions, confuses speed with clarity, micromanages, treats disagreement as disloyalty, claims disproportionate credit.
- **Underexpressed:** avoids necessary decisions, waits for permission despite responsibility, hides behind consensus, fails to set boundaries or standards.

## Common triggers
Ambiguous authority, slow group decisions, visible failure, public challenge, low trust, repeated rework, and responsibility without control.

## Protective factors
Decision-right matrices, pre-mortems, dissent rounds, delegation contracts, after-action reviews, explicit ethics checks, and feedback from people affected by the decision.

## Leadership tests for app prompts
- Did you define the problem before directing the solution?
- Whose information is missing?
- What can be delegated with clear outcomes?
- Are you protecting standards or protecting ego?
- Who should receive credit?

## Safety rules
No prediction of rank, political power, business success or guaranteed authority. No labels such as dictator, tyrant, narcissist or weak leader from birth date alone.

## Growth target
From control-centred leadership to accountable, inclusive and enabling leadership.
