# M1-R007 — Ambition and Achievement Deep Dive

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R007

## Scope
Traditional Number 1 descriptions often emphasize ambition, advancement and recognition. This unit converts those themes into a non-deterministic achievement model.

## Dimensions
- goal clarity
- mastery motivation
- status motivation
- persistence
- delayed gratification
- progress monitoring
- response to setbacks
- sustainable pacing
- ethical achievement
- identity beyond performance

## Expression model
**Balanced:** chooses meaningful goals, converts them into milestones, persists while evidence supports the goal, learns from setbacks, and separates personal worth from outcomes.

**Overextended:** makes achievement the main source of worth, pursues status without fit, overworks, competes unnecessarily, hides uncertainty, or continues a failing path to protect pride.

**Underexpressed:** avoids ambitious goals due to fear of judgment, delays starting until certainty is impossible, minimizes achievements, or abandons goals after early difficulty.

## Triggers
Comparison, public evaluation, blocked promotion, loss of status, slow progress, dependence on gatekeepers, and visible failure.

## Protective factors
Values-based goals, process metrics, recovery time, stop/revise criteria, accountability, multiple identity roles, and celebrating learning as well as outcomes.

## App prompts
- Is this goal meaningful to you or mainly impressive to others?
- What evidence would justify continuing, revising or stopping?
- What pace can you sustain for six months?
- What remains valuable about you if this goal fails?

## Safety
Do not promise fame, wealth, office, promotion or entrepreneurial success. Do not frame lower ambition as weakness.

## Growth target
Purposeful achievement: high standards with flexibility, ethics, recovery and self-worth independent of rank.
