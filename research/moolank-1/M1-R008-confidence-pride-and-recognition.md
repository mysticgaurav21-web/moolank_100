## 1. Purpose

This record converts recurring Number 1 themes of confidence, pride, dignity, visibility, status and recognition into a structured, conditional and app-safe model.

It does not claim that a person born on 1, 10, 19 or 28 has a scientifically demonstrated level of confidence, self-esteem, pride, sensitivity to criticism or desire for recognition. Birth-number personality prediction remains scientifically unestablished.

The app should use this domain as a reflective lens for questions such as:

- How accurately does the user estimate capability?
- How comfortably can the user be visible without exaggeration or self-erasure?
- How does the user receive praise, credit, criticism and correction?
- Is self-worth stable, or heavily dependent on achievement, rank or approval?
- Can pride support dignity and effort without becoming superiority or image protection?
- Can the user recognise others without experiencing their success as personal diminishment?

## 2. Core distinctions

The app must keep the following constructs separate.

### 2.1 Confidence

Confidence is a broad everyday term for trust in one’s judgment, capability or ability to respond. It should not be treated as a single permanent trait.

### 2.2 Self-efficacy

Self-efficacy concerns belief in capability for a specific task or situation. It is domain-sensitive. A person may feel effective in business negotiation and uncertain in emotional communication.

### 2.3 Self-esteem

Self-esteem refers to broader evaluation or regard for the self. It is not identical to skill confidence, public visibility or pride in a specific achievement.

### 2.4 Self-respect

Self-respect concerns dignity, standards and the belief that one deserves fair treatment. It can remain intact even when performance is imperfect.

### 2.5 Pride

Pride may describe satisfaction in effort, values, identity or achievement. It can function constructively when linked to specific actions and shared reality. It becomes less useful when it shifts toward superiority, entitlement or protection of a flawless image.

### 2.6 Recognition

Recognition is acknowledgement of contribution, effort, presence, expertise or role. Wanting recognition is not automatically vanity. Problems arise when recognition becomes the main proof of worth or when lack of recognition triggers coercion, retaliation or collapse.

### 2.7 Status

Status is relative social standing, influence or prestige within a group. Status preference must not be treated as equivalent to competence, moral value or leadership capacity.

### 2.8 Humility

Humility is not self-belittlement. In this database it means accurate self-appraisal, openness to correction, awareness of limits and willingness to recognise others.

### 2.9 Shame and embarrassment

Embarrassment may follow a social mistake or exposure. Shame is a broader painful evaluation of the self. The app must not diagnose shame disorders or infer trauma from birth date.

### 2.10 Defensiveness

Defensiveness is a response pattern in which information is resisted because it threatens identity, status, belonging or control. It is not proof of narcissism or personality disorder.

## 3. Traditional Number 1 proposition

Modern Number 1 traditions commonly associate the number with individuality, authority, ambition, visible responsibility, definite views, dignity, pride and desire for recognition or leadership.

Canonical traditional formulation:

> Number 1 tradition emphasises self-definition, visible authorship, dignity and acknowledgement of contribution.

App-safe formulation:

> You may find it useful to reflect on how you express confidence, respond to praise or criticism, and balance healthy pride with openness to feedback. Your actual pattern depends on experience, skill, relationships, culture and context—not your birth number alone.

## 4. Confidence–pride–recognition architecture

The domain is divided into twelve dimensions.

## 4.1 Capability calibration

Ability to estimate what one can currently do, what remains uncertain and what support is required.

### Balanced expression

- confidence is linked to relevant evidence, preparation and experience;
- distinguishes familiarity from mastery;
- can state both strengths and limits;
- updates confidence after feedback;
- uses small tests where uncertainty is high.

### Overextended expression

- treats certainty of tone as evidence of correctness;
- underestimates complexity or missing information;
- assumes past success transfers automatically to a new domain;
- resists expert input because it threatens image;
- makes high-cost commitments before testing assumptions.

### Underexpressed expression

- discounts genuine competence;
- waits for total certainty before acting;
- treats discomfort as proof of inability;
- needs repeated reassurance for familiar tasks;
- generalises one failure into global incapability.

### App prompts

- What evidence supports your confidence in this specific task?
- What important uncertainty remains?
- What small test would improve calibration?

## 4.2 Visibility tolerance

Capacity to be seen, evaluated and associated with one’s work without exaggeration or disappearance.

### Balanced expression

- accepts appropriate visibility and accountability;
- presents contribution accurately;
- can speak publicly while acknowledging collaboration;
- tolerates being evaluated without treating evaluation as total identity;
- chooses visibility according to purpose rather than compulsion.

### Overextended expression

- seeks attention disconnected from contribution;
- amplifies certainty or achievement to maintain image;
- treats every group setting as a stage for status;
- interrupts or recentres discussion;
- experiences another person’s visibility as displacement.

### Underexpressed expression

- hides useful work;
- minimises achievement before others can evaluate it;
- avoids applying, presenting or leading despite readiness;
- apologises for taking legitimate space;
- attributes success entirely to luck while ignoring effort and skill.

### App prompts

- What useful contribution becomes possible if you are visible?
- Are you seeking attention, accountability, opportunity or reassurance?
- What level of visibility is proportionate to the purpose?

## 4.3 Receiving praise

Ability to accept positive feedback without dependence, dismissal or inflation.

### Balanced expression

- receives praise without immediate self-denial;
- asks for specificity when useful;
- appreciates recognition while retaining internal standards;
- shares credit where appropriate;
- does not require praise to continue every valued action.

### Overextended expression

- repeatedly seeks reassurance after praise fades;
- treats praise as confirmation of superiority;
- becomes dependent on external applause;
- selectively listens only to positive feedback;
- escalates self-presentation to maintain admiration.

### Underexpressed expression

- rejects compliments automatically;
- assumes praise is politeness or manipulation;
- feels exposed when acknowledged;
- redirects all credit even when contribution was real;
- cannot integrate positive evidence into self-assessment.

### App prompts

- Can you receive this praise as information rather than a verdict?
- Which part reflects your effort, skill, support or circumstance?
- Is there credit that should be shared?

## 4.4 Recognition need and recognition dependence

Relationship between acknowledgement and motivation or self-worth.

### Balanced expression

- values fair acknowledgement;
- communicates credit concerns directly;
- can continue meaningful work without constant visibility;
- distinguishes appreciation from entitlement;
- recognises contributions reciprocally.

### Overextended expression

- treats delayed acknowledgement as disrespect;
- withdraws effort when not central;
- competes for ownership of shared success;
- monitors relative attention excessively;
- uses anger, silence or pressure to obtain recognition.

### Underexpressed expression

- never asks for fair credit;
- allows repeated erasure of contribution;
- interprets self-advocacy as arrogance;
- accepts exploitative invisibility;
- loses motivation but does not communicate the need.

### App prompts

- What acknowledgement would be fair and specific?
- Is the need for credit practical, relational, reputational or identity-based?
- How can you request recognition without devaluing others?

## 4.5 Response to criticism

Capacity to evaluate corrective information without automatic submission or rejection.

### Balanced expression

- separates the message from the delivery where possible;
- asks for examples and impact;
- checks whether the source has relevant information;
- accepts accurate feedback without global self-condemnation;
- rejects inaccurate feedback with reasons rather than retaliation.

### Overextended expression

- treats correction as disrespect or disloyalty;
- argues before understanding the evidence;
- attacks the source instead of evaluating content;
- protects status by concealing mistakes;
- demands agreement as proof of respect.

### Underexpressed expression

- accepts criticism without checking accuracy;
- converts behaviour feedback into global inadequacy;
- becomes silent, ashamed or avoidant;
- abandons useful roles after minor correction;
- assumes authority figures are always correct.

### App prompts

- What specific behaviour or outcome is being discussed?
- What part may be accurate, incomplete or inaccurate?
- What response protects both learning and dignity?

## 4.6 Public correction and embarrassment recovery

Ability to recover when an error, limitation or disagreement becomes visible.

### Balanced expression

- pauses before reacting;
- acknowledges factual error proportionately;
- repairs impact when needed;
- continues participating after correction;
- reviews what the event means without turning it into identity collapse.

### Overextended expression

- retaliates against the corrector;
- shifts blame immediately;
- doubles down to avoid loss of face;
- attempts to dominate the room after embarrassment;
- later rewrites the event to protect image.

### Underexpressed expression

- withdraws from the group or role;
- replays the event excessively;
- avoids future visibility;
- assumes everyone now sees the whole self as incompetent;
- over-apologises beyond the actual impact.

### App prompts

- What exactly happened, without adding a global identity judgment?
- Is repair, clarification, boundary-setting or simple continuation needed?
- What would proportionate recovery look like within twenty-four hours?

## 4.7 Earned pride

Capacity to experience satisfaction in effort, values, contribution or achievement.

### Balanced expression

- pride is linked to specific actions or values;
- recognises effort, support and circumstance;
- allows satisfaction without superiority;
- uses pride to reinforce meaningful behaviour;
- celebrates others without losing one’s own accomplishment.

### Overextended expression

- turns achievement into proof of higher human value;
- exaggerates personal authorship;
- uses success to justify dominance;
- protects a superior identity at the cost of truth;
- becomes contemptuous toward people with different outcomes.

### Underexpressed expression

- refuses to acknowledge progress;
- treats satisfaction as vanity;
- immediately raises standards so success cannot register;
- minimises contribution;
- cannot pause long enough to consolidate learning.

### App prompts

- What specific effort, value or skill are you proud of?
- Who or what supported the outcome?
- How can pride strengthen contribution rather than comparison?

## 4.8 Humility and teachability

Ability to hold a realistic sense of strength while remaining open to limits, uncertainty and influence.

### Balanced expression

- can say “I know,” “I do not know” and “I was wrong” accurately;
- seeks input from people with relevant information;
- changes position when evidence changes;
- remains willing to learn from lower-status people;
- recognises luck, timing and structural support.

### Overextended expression

- performs humility to gain admiration;
- minimises language while privately rejecting all input;
- uses expertise as immunity from correction;
- equates rank with truth;
- treats uncertainty as weakness.

### Underexpressed expression

- self-belittles;
- hides competence to reduce threat;
- gives away authority that belongs to the role;
- avoids making claims even when evidence is strong;
- confuses humility with passivity.

### App prompts

- What do you know with reasonable confidence?
- What remains unknown or outside your expertise?
- Whose perspective could materially improve the decision?

## 4.9 Internal validation

Ability to evaluate effort and conduct using evidence and values without relying entirely on external reaction.

### Balanced expression

- uses personally endorsed standards;
- records evidence of progress;
- can remain committed during periods of low recognition;
- distinguishes social feedback from total worth;
- revises internal standards when they are unrealistic or unethical.

### Overextended expression

- dismisses all external feedback under the label of self-validation;
- treats personal conviction as sufficient evidence;
- refuses accountability;
- uses “I know my worth” to avoid examining impact;
- idealises independence from social reality.

### Underexpressed expression

- cannot assess work until others react;
- changes self-view rapidly with praise or criticism;
- relies on comparison for motivation;
- abandons values to preserve approval;
- repeatedly seeks reassurance after decisions.

### App prompts

- What standards matter even when nobody is watching?
- What external feedback is still decision-relevant?
- Are your internal standards humane, evidence-based and revisable?

## 4.10 Reciprocal recognition

Capacity to notice, name and credit the contribution of others.

### Balanced expression

- gives specific acknowledgement;
- distributes credit accurately;
- distinguishes leadership from sole authorship;
- celebrates others without forced self-comparison;
- corrects omissions when discovered.

### Overextended expression

- praises strategically to control loyalty;
- gives recognition only when it reinforces hierarchy;
- withholds credit from perceived rivals;
- recentres another person’s achievement around oneself;
- uses public praise while privately undermining autonomy.

### Underexpressed expression

- assumes contribution is obvious and need not be named;
- feels awkward expressing appreciation;
- takes support for granted;
- focuses only on errors;
- withholds praise through fear it reduces personal authority.

### App prompts

- Whose contribution materially affected this outcome?
- What specific acknowledgement is due?
- Can you recognise another person without adding a comparison to yourself?

## 4.11 Status comparison and rank sensitivity

Response to relative position, prestige, visibility or influence.

### Balanced expression

- notices status dynamics without making them the sole measure of worth;
- advocates for fair role recognition;
- can follow someone better placed to lead;
- uses comparison selectively for learning;
- maintains dignity across rank differences.

### Overextended expression

- monitors who is ahead, central or admired;
- treats status loss as personal humiliation;
- devalues lower-status people;
- pursues titles detached from responsibility;
- converts collaboration into competition.

### Underexpressed expression

- avoids legitimate advancement;
- feels unworthy around high-status people;
- tolerates disrespect because of hierarchy;
- hides ambition;
- assumes rank proves superior competence or moral value.

### App prompts

- What does this status difference actually change?
- Are you seeking role clarity, influence, security, admiration or fairness?
- What remains true about your worth regardless of rank?

## 4.12 Self-worth beyond performance and image

Capacity to maintain humane self-regard when outcomes, praise or status fluctuate.

### Balanced expression

- sees achievement as meaningful but not total identity;
- retains dignity after failure;
- maintains multiple identity domains;
- can repair mistakes without self-annihilation;
- values character, relationships, health and contribution alongside performance.

### Overextended expression

- protects image at any cost;
- hides losses or mistakes;
- overworks to prevent status decline;
- treats ordinary imperfection as unacceptable;
- uses achievement to avoid vulnerability.

### Underexpressed expression

- interprets setbacks as proof of worthlessness;
- withdraws from opportunity after criticism;
- allows others’ judgments to define identity;
- cannot remember strengths during failure;
- abandons self-care when disappointed.

### App prompts

- What remains valuable about you if this outcome changes?
- Which identities and relationships exist beyond performance?
- What would accountability without self-rejection look like?

## 5. Functional state model

### 5.1 Calibrated confidence

The person uses evidence, preparation and context to estimate capability, communicates clearly, remains open to correction and updates judgment when needed.

### 5.2 Image-protective confidence

The person uses certainty, dominance, self-promotion or resistance to feedback to defend status or identity. The primary goal may shift from solving the problem to avoiding visible limitation.

### 5.3 Inhibited confidence

The person has relevant capability but suppresses visibility, discounts evidence, overweights criticism or waits for reassurance before acting.

### 5.4 Recognition-dependent state

Motivation and self-worth fluctuate sharply with attention, praise, credit or rank. The person may compete for visibility or withdraw when acknowledgement is absent.

### 5.5 Restored confidence

The person separates behaviour from identity, reviews evidence, names limits, repairs impact, accepts support and chooses the next proportionate action.

- unfair credit distribution;
- another person receiving more attention;
- visible failure after a confident claim;
- status loss or demotion;
- rejection after self-promotion;
- comparison with a peer;

The app must not infer any trigger from birth number. Triggers are hypotheses to validate through user report and context.

- preparation and skill development;
- stable relationships not based solely on performance;
- private progress measures;
- behaviour-specific feedback;
- permission to revise publicly;
- fair credit systems;
- self-compassion without denial of responsibility;
- multiple identity roles;
- reciprocal appreciation;

- transparent promotion and evaluation criteria;
- recovery time after public stress;
- explicit distinction between role, performance and human worth;
- environments where uncertainty can be acknowledged safely;

## 9. Psychology boundary layer

Psychology is used to define constructs and safe practices. It does not validate numerology.

### 9.1 Self-efficacy boundary

Self-efficacy should be assessed for specific tasks. The app must avoid global labels such as “high confidence person” based only on one domain or on birth date.

App implications:

- ask what task is involved;
- review past performance and current preparation;
- distinguish confidence from competence;
- include support and environmental constraints;
- update confidence after evidence.

### 9.2 Self-esteem boundary

Broad self-esteem is not identical to public assertiveness. A person may speak confidently while holding unstable self-worth, or appear quiet while maintaining strong self-respect.

App implications:

- do not infer self-esteem from volume, dominance or visibility;
- do not label low confidence as low worth;
- distinguish temporary disappointment from global self-evaluation.

### 9.3 Contingent self-worth

Self-worth may become tied to domains such as achievement, approval, appearance, competition or status. This can produce unstable motivation and defensive responses when the domain is threatened.

App implications:

- ask what event seems to prove about the self;
- expand identity beyond one performance domain;
- preserve accountability while challenging totalising conclusions;
- avoid promising permanent confidence through numerology.

### 9.4 Pride distinction

Research literature often distinguishes pride linked to specific effort or accomplishment from pride linked to global superiority. The app should not diagnose either form. It should translate the distinction behaviourally.

App implications:

- link appreciation to specific effort, value or contribution;
- avoid superiority claims;
- include support, luck and context;
- monitor whether pride increases contribution or contempt.

### 9.5 Self-compassion boundary

Self-compassion can support recovery after mistakes when it includes accurate awareness, common humanity and a constructive response. It is not denial, excuse-making or avoidance of repair.

App implications:

- name the mistake accurately;
- identify impact;
- separate behaviour from total worth;
- choose repair and learning;
- prohibit use as a substitute for professional support where needed.

### 9.6 Social comparison boundary

Comparison can provide information, but repeated rank-focused comparison may distort self-evaluation and motivation.

App implications:

- ask whether comparison is being used for learning, fairness, motivation or worth;
- choose process and personal-baseline metrics;
- reduce exposure when comparison is dysregulating;
- do not claim Number 1 people are naturally more competitive.

## 12. Growth practices

These are educational practices, not clinical treatment.

### 12.1 Confidence evidence ledger

For one domain, record:

- relevant successful experiences;
- current skills;
- preparation completed;
- missing knowledge;
- support available;
- cost of error;
- next safe test.

Use the ledger to produce a calibrated statement:

> I am prepared for X, uncertain about Y and will reduce risk through Z.

### 12.2 Strength–limit statement

Complete:

> A current strength I can use is ____. A limit I should respect or address is ____.

The practice prevents both exaggeration and self-erasure.

### 12.3 Praise integration protocol

When receiving praise:

1. pause;
2. say thank you;
3. identify the specific behaviour or contribution;
4. note support or collaboration where relevant;
5. avoid immediate dismissal or inflation.

### 12.4 Feedback triage

Separate feedback into:

- factual observation;
- interpretation;
- impact;
- request;
- source credibility;
- action required.

Respond after evaluating each component.

### 12.5 Public-error recovery

1. regulate immediate activation;
2. state the factual correction;
3. acknowledge impact if any;
4. clarify the revised action;
5. continue participation;
6. review privately later.

### 12.6 Recognition reciprocity

Once per week, name one specific contribution from another person without adding self-comparison, strategic flattery or vague praise.

### 12.7 Identity portfolio

List at least five valued identity domains, such as:

- work or craft;
- relationships;
- learning;
- health;
- creativity;
- service;
- spirituality;
- community;
- play or rest.

Review whether one domain has become the sole source of worth.

### 12.8 Status-to-purpose reset

When status comparison rises, ask:

- What is the actual purpose?
- What contribution is needed?
- What information does the comparison provide?
- What part is merely rank anxiety?
- What action would still matter without public recognition?

## 17. Source registry

### T-M1-001 — Cheiro, *Cheiro’s Book of Numbers*, Number 1 section

**Use:** Traditional source for individuality, ambition, authority, definite views and visible leadership orientation.

**Limitations:** Occult and anecdotal framework; no controlled evidence; absolute claims must not be copied into app language.

### P-SE-001 — Albert Bandura, self-efficacy framework

**Use:** Domain-specific capability beliefs, calibration and mastery boundaries.

**Limitations:** Does not validate numerology or global confidence labels.

### P-SDT-001 — Self-Determination Theory

**Use:** Internalisation, autonomy, competence and the difference between self-endorsed motivation and approval dependence.

**Limitations:** Does not establish birth-number effects.

### P-CSW-001 — Contingencies of self-worth research

**Use:** Boundary for self-worth tied to approval, competition, achievement or other domains.

**Limitations:** Individual differences require validated assessment and context; not a numerology validation source.

### P-PRIDE-001 — Authentic and hubristic pride research family

**Use:** Supports behavioural distinction between pride linked to specific accomplishment and pride linked to global superiority.

**Limitations:** Labels should not be assigned diagnostically to users; research models and measurement debates must be acknowledged.

### P-SC-001 — Self-compassion research family

**Use:** Recovery after mistakes, separating behaviour from total worth and maintaining constructive accountability.

**Limitations:** General educational use only; not a replacement for clinical care.

### P-COMP-001 — Social comparison research family

**Use:** Understanding rank sensitivity, informational comparison and comparison-related self-evaluation.

**Limitations:** Effects depend on direction, domain, culture, motivation and context.

## 18. Source conflicts and limitations

- Traditional numerology sources often use pride, ego, confidence, dignity, authority and leadership interchangeably.
- Popular sources frequently repeat the same claims without independent evidence.
- High visibility may reflect role demands, culture, learned communication or social privilege rather than confidence.
- Quietness does not prove low confidence; assertiveness does not prove secure self-worth.
- Wanting recognition can reflect fairness, role clarity, attachment needs, career requirements or identity threat.
- Pride research uses multiple models; the app should prefer observable behaviour over fixed labels.
- Self-report is vulnerable to impression management, mood and cultural norms.
- Birth-date reduction has no established predictive validity for confidence, pride, recognition need or status sensitivity.
