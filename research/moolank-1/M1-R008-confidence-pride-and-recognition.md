# M1-R008 — Confidence, Pride and Recognition Deep Dive

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R008
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved

## Core distinctions
Confidence is a calibrated belief in capability; self-esteem is broader self-evaluation; pride can reflect earned satisfaction or defensive status protection; recognition is social acknowledgement. None is inferred as a fixed trait from birth date.

## Dimensions
- calibrated confidence
- willingness to be visible
- receiving praise
- recognition sensitivity
- response to criticism
- repair after embarrassment
- humility
- self-validation

## Expression model
**Balanced:** communicates capability without exaggeration, accepts praise, acknowledges limits, uses criticism as information, and recognizes others.

**Overextended:** overclaims certainty, protects image, dismisses criticism, competes for attention, or treats recognition as proof of worth.

**Underexpressed:** hides contribution, rejects praise, apologizes for visibility, defers despite competence, or interprets mistakes as global inadequacy.

## Triggers
Public correction, being ignored, unfair credit distribution, comparison, failure after confident claims, and status loss.

## Protective factors
Specific feedback, evidence logs, private correction where possible, self-compassion, reciprocal recognition, and separating behaviour from identity.

## App prompts
- What evidence supports your confidence?
- Can you name one limit without diminishing your strengths?
- Are you seeking useful acknowledgement or reassurance about worth?
- Whose contribution should you recognize?

## Safety
Never infer narcissism, superiority, inferiority, fragile ego or personality disorder. Avoid flattery designed to create dependence.

## Growth target
Quiet confidence: visible, evidence-based, receptive to feedback and generous with recognition.
