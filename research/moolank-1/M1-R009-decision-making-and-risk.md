# M1-R009 — Decision-Making and Risk Deep Dive

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R009
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved

## Scope
Traditional Number 1 language often emphasizes decisiveness and forward movement. The app must separate decision speed from decision quality.

## Dimensions
- problem definition
- information sufficiency
- option generation
- uncertainty tolerance
- reversibility assessment
- risk/reward calibration
- consultation
- commitment
- review and revision

## Expression model
**Balanced:** defines the decision, gathers decision-relevant information, distinguishes reversible from irreversible choices, acts at an appropriate threshold, and revises when evidence changes.

**Overextended:** acts to end discomfort, equates delay with weakness, ignores base rates, underestimates downside, or refuses revision to preserve authority.

**Underexpressed:** seeks impossible certainty, overconsults, avoids ownership, delays low-risk choices, or repeatedly reopens settled decisions.

## Decision protocol
1. What exactly must be decided?
2. What happens if no decision is made?
3. Is it reversible?
4. Which facts would materially change the choice?
5. Who is affected and should be consulted?
6. What is the downside limit?
7. When will the decision be reviewed?

## Triggers
Urgency, public pressure, challenged authority, competition, incomplete control, and fear of appearing indecisive.

## Safety
No financial, medical or legal recommendation may be generated from Moolank. High-stakes decisions require domain-qualified advice.

## Growth target
Decisive flexibility: timely action with evidence, consultation, downside awareness and willingness to revise.
