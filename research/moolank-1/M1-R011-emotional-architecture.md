# M1-R011 — Emotional Architecture

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R011
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved

## Scope
This unit maps emotional awareness and regulation themes without diagnosing temperament or mental health from a birth number.

## Dimensions
Emotional noticing, naming, expression, vulnerability, anger, frustration, disappointment, shame, pride-related hurt, loneliness, support seeking, recovery and meaning-making.

## Balanced expression
- notices emotion before acting;
- names needs without blaming;
- tolerates vulnerability while maintaining boundaries;
- distinguishes criticism of behaviour from rejection of self;
- seeks support appropriately;
- recovers through reflection, rest, repair and renewed action.

## Under pressure
Possible patterns include irritability when blocked, defensiveness after public correction, emotional compression, rapid problem-solving instead of listening, withdrawal after failure, or using work and control to avoid vulnerable feelings.

## Protective factors
Emotion-labeling, body check-ins, delayed response, private reflection, supportive conversation, sleep and recovery, self-compassion, and repair scripts.

## Reflection prompts
- What emotion is present beneath irritation?
- What need or value feels threatened?
- Do you need action, acceptance, reassurance or rest?
- What would a proportionate response look like tomorrow?

## Safety
No inference of depression, anxiety disorder, trauma, bipolar disorder, personality disorder or emotional instability. Crisis content must route to appropriate support systems.

## Growth target
Emotionally informed agency: feelings become useful information without becoming commands or identity.
