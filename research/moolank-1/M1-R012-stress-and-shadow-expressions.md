# M1-R012 — Stress and Shadow Expressions

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R012
- Status: Research complete; canonical extraction approved

## Definition
“Shadow” means a context-dependent overextension, inhibition or protective habit—not evil, diagnosis or fixed character.

## Stress triggers
Blocked autonomy, loss of control, humiliation, delayed progress, dependency, unfair credit, repeated failure, unclear authority, excessive workload and inability to influence outcomes.

## Possible stress expressions
- impatience and command behaviour;
- rigid persistence after a goal stops being useful;
- overwork and reduced recovery;
- refusal of help;
- criticism sensitivity;
- blaming or defensive certainty;
- withdrawal after visible failure;
- escalating control when trust is low.

## Recovery sequence
1. Reduce immediate activation.
2. Name the trigger and threatened need.
3. Separate controllable, influenceable and uncontrollable factors.
4. Request information or support.
5. Choose repair, revision, delegation or rest.
6. Review the pattern after recovery.

## Protective factors
Predictable routines, decision boundaries, realistic workloads, peer feedback, restorative sleep, movement, emotion regulation, multiple sources of identity and explicit stop rules.

## Safety
Do not infer violence, addiction, criminality, psychiatric disorder or abuse. Behavioural risk must be assessed from actual conduct and context.

## Growth target
Flexible strength: retaining initiative while tolerating limits, uncertainty, support and revision.
