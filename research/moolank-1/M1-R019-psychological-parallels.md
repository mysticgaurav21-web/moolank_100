# M1-R019 — Psychological Parallels and Scientific Boundary

## Metadata
- Record ID: M1-R019

## Boundary statement
Psychology can define constructs relevant to app reflection, but it does not validate that birth-day reduction predicts those constructs. `scientificBirthNumberLink` remains `not-established`.

## Construct map
- initiative → proactive behaviour and action initiation
- agency → perceived capacity to influence action
- autonomy → volition and self-endorsement
- confidence → calibrated domain-specific belief
- self-efficacy → belief about carrying out a particular action
- ambition → goal importance, attainment and status motives
- persistence → continued effort with adaptive revision
- assertiveness → direct, respectful expression
- leadership → influence process within a group and context
- recognition sensitivity → response to social evaluation
- flexibility → revising behaviour when circumstances change

## Non-equivalences
- autonomy is not isolation;
- confidence is not superiority;
- leadership is not dominance;
- persistence is not stubbornness;
- assertiveness is not aggression;
- self-efficacy is not guaranteed performance;
- recognition need is not narcissism.

## App use
Psychology may support definitions, questionnaires, reflection prompts and low-risk skill practices. It must not be cited as evidence that Moolank 1 causes or predicts personality.

## Questionnaire rule
Responses measure current self-report and context. They do not prove the numerology profile and must allow contradiction, change over time and “not applicable.”

## Barnum protection
Use specific behavioural questions, counterexamples, conditional language and user-controlled relevance ratings. Avoid universally flattering statements.

## Safety
No diagnosis, treatment claim, psychometric validity claim or scientific endorsement of numerology.
